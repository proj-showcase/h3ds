Overview

Abstract

Manual rendering of 3D indoor scenes is a time- consuming and labor-intensive process, yet the demand for these environments in the metaverse, 3D animation, and virtual reality has driven significant research inter- est in their automatic generation. To address this chal- lenge, we propose a novel framework that uses natural language instructions to automatically generate 3D in- door environments and integrate them with VR devices.

Our framework enables human-robot collaboration, allowing robots to perform household tasks while humans interact with objects and perceive state changes through a haptic glove and head-mounted displays. The system fine-tunes LLMs with Low-Rank Adaptation to extract a subgraph that captures key objects and their spatial relationships. From this subgraph, a dedicated VR de- vice position prediction module determines the optimal placement of VR devices. Our method has achieved state-of-the-art results on four tasks of the ALFRED dataset, suggesting its effectiveness and potential applications.
